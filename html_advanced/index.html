Introduction

Arts in education is an expanding field of educational research and practice informed by investigations into learning through arts experiences. In this context, the arts can include Performing arts education (dance, drama, music), literature and poetry, storytelling, Visual arts education in film, craft, design, digital arts, media and photography. It is distinguished from art education by being not so much about teaching art, but focused on:

how to improve learning through the arts how to transfer learning in and through the arts to other disciplines discovering and creating understanding of human behavior, thinking, potential, and learning especially through the close observation of works of art and various forms of involvement in arts experiences

Projects

The European Union has funded the ARTinED (a new approach to education using the arts) project to integrate the arts into every primary school subject ARTinED. The Fondazione Nazionale Carlo Collodi is the project coordinator of the ARTinED project.

Importance

The benefits of arts education programs in schools can also extend beyond therapeutic practices. Involvement in the arts can lead to increased academic performance.

for example;

students who participate in an arts centered program show increased performance in both verbal and mathematical assessments (Vaughn and Winner, 2000).

No Child Left Behind Act (NCLB):

This program requires that arts education, visual and performing, be considered core subjects as they have been proven by many tests to work.

Art programs within education has its importance in not only expanding the mind but keeping kids off the streets and out of the correctional system.

"students with art programs are three times more likely to graduate than those who don't".

History and classifications

Classifications

The arts have also been classified as seven: painting, architecture, sculpture, literature, music, performing and cinema.

Painting

is the practice of applying paint, pigment, color or other medium to a solid surface (called the "matrix" or "support").

Architecture

This is the art and technique of designing and building, as distinguished from the skills associated with construction.

Sculpture

Sculpture is the three-dimensional art work which is physically presented in the dimensions of height, width and depth.

Visual arts

Architecture

Architecture is the art and science of designing buildings and structures.

Ceramics

Ceramic art is art made from ceramic materials (including clay), which may take forms such as pottery, tile, figurines, sculpture, and tableware.

Conceptual art

Conceptual art is art wherein the concept(s) or idea(s) involved in the work take precedence over traditional aesthetic and material concerns.

Drawing

Drawing is a means of making an image, using any of a wide variety of tools and techniques.
